Our services

The Gonstead System

Step 1:
History taking

Our health problems are an accumulation of all things life puts us through. By asking the right questions, our chiropractors will piece together the puzzle that explains what caused said health issue, and decide the best way to approach your case. A complete health and medical history helps the chiropractor and the patient understand what may be the root cause of the problem so that it can be avoided down the road of recovery.

Step 2:

Step 3:
Visualization

From the moment the patient steps into the treatment room, the chiropractor has been observing multiple aspects of him or her. How they walk, how they sit, if they're leaning to one side, etc. This is another step in collecting information about where the actual problem is. By observing the spine, the chiropractor is able to pick up imbalances of muscle, and the general symmetry of the patient's posture.

Step 4:
Palpation

The spine consists of multiple segments, and each of these segments are meant to move a certain degree and direction. However, with a misalignment, this movement is limited, and with it usually comes with the evidence of swelling e.g. pain, tightness. Palpation involves the chiropractor feeling and identifying each individual segment in the spine and pelvis and determining which bone doesn't function as they should.
